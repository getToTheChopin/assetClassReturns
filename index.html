Historical Investment Returns by Asset Class

 

This tool shows the historical investment returns of a broad set of "asset classes" -- stocks, bonds, and beyond.

Understanding the potential range of returns on your investments is a critical input to any financial plan or decision, whether that's renting versus buying your home, forecasting your time to retirement, or just getting a general grip on where your financial trajectory is headed.

For those who've done some digging into historical investment returns, you've likely come across performance charts for the S&P 500 -- an index that tracks the performance of the 500 largest publicly-traded companies in the U.S.

While the S&P 500 index covers the companies that feature prominently in business news (Apple, Amazon, Microsoft, Google, JP Morgan, and the like), there's a whole wide world out there!

Broadly speaking, the 'universe' of investment options can be broken down into the following:

  • Underlying asset type -- whether that's stocks, bonds / fixed income, or other assets such as real estate, hard assets, commodities, etc.
  • Country -- developed countries (such as the U.S. / Canada / Germany) versus emerging markets (such as China / India / Brazil / Russia)
  • Risk level -- stocks of large mature companies versus small growing firms, government bonds versus higher risk / higher yield corporate bonds

Paul Simon sang about the "50 ways to leave your lover", but surely he forgot about the 100+ ways to build a portfolio...

In this analysis, we'll stretch ourselves beyond the S&P 500, and take into account the following asset classes.
